# Diabetes-data-analysis
This project involves analyzing health-related data to investigate the relationship between certain health conditions and the likelihood of diabetes. The focus is on visualizing these relationships using various plots, such as bar plots, and interpreting the results.
The objective is to explore how factors like high blood pressure (HighBP), high cholesterol (HighChol), and other health indicators correlate with diabetes occurrence (Diabetes_binary), which is typically a binary outcome (1 = diabetic, 0 = non-diabetic)
Key Insights:
Relationships: The main goal of this project is to explore how factors like high cholesterol and high blood pressure impact the likelihood of diabetes.
Data Visualization: The bar plots make it easy to see whether conditions like high cholesterol or blood pressure are associated with a higher or lower risk of diabetes. If the average diabetes outcome for individuals with high cholesterol (or high blood pressure) is significantly higher, it might indicate a potential risk factor.
Why This Project is Important:
Health Risk Factors: Identifying correlations between high cholesterol, high blood pressure, and diabetes can help improve early detection and prevention efforts.
Public Health: It can inform healthcare providers about the importance of monitoring these factors for individuals at risk of diabetes.
Predictive Analysis: It lays the foundation for using machine learning models to predict the likelihood of diabetes based on these health conditions.
This project provides an exploratory data analysis (EDA) of a health dataset, focusing on visualizing the relationship between certain health conditions (like high cholesterol and high blood pressure) and the likelihood of diabetes.
